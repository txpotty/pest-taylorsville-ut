Subterranean Termite Treatment

Subterranean termites are the most common and destructive species in Utah. Our treatments for subterranean termites often involve creating a barrier around your property to prevent them from entering. This can be achieved through:

  • Liquid Termiticides: We apply a liquid product to the soil around the foundation, creating a treated zone that termites cannot cross. Modern termiticides are designed to be non-repellent, meaning termites won't detect them and will unknowingly come into contact with the product, spreading it throughout the colony.
  • Termite Baiting Systems: These systems involve strategically placing bait stations around your property. Termites consume the bait, which contains a slow-acting ingredient that is carried back to the colony, eventually eliminating it. Baiting systems offer a non-disruptive and environmentally friendly option.

The choice between liquid treatments and baiting systems depends on various factors, including the severity of the infestation, the type of soil, and your personal preferences. Our experts will recommend the most suitable option for your situation.

Drywood Termite Treatment

  • Fumigation: In severe infestations, fumigation may be necessary to eliminate termites throughout the entire structure.
  • Localized Treatments: For smaller infestations, we can use spot treatments with termiticides injected directly into infested wood.

Diligent and Meticulous Recognizing the Signs: Do You Need Termite Treatment?

Many homeowners in Taylorsville, UT are unaware they have a termite problem until the damage is significant. Being proactive in recognizing the signs can save you considerable expense and stress down the line. Termites are often called 'silent destroyers' because they can work unseen for years, consuming the cellulose in wood, which is a primary component of your home's structure.

Common Signs of Termite Infestation

  • Mud Tubes: These are the most tell-tale sign of subterranean termites. They build these tubes from soil and saliva to travel between their underground colony and their food source (your home's wood) while protecting themselves from open air. Look for these pencil-width tubes on your foundation walls, crawl spaces, or along beams.

    • Finding mud tubes is a strong indicator of active subterranean termite activity. disturbing a tube might reveal live termites inside. Ignoring these can lead to widespread damage throughout your home’s wooden elements.

  • Damaged Wood: Termite-damaged wood may appear blistered or dark. When tapped, it may sound hollow because termites consume the interior structure of the wood while leaving the exterior shell intact. Probe suspicious areas with a screwdriver to check for softness or hollowness.

    • Unlike rot, termite damage typically follows the grain of the wood, creating galleries or hollowed-out pathways. Early detection of damaged wood can significantly limit the extent of repairs needed later.

  • Termite Swarmers and Discarded Wings: Reproductive termites, also known as swarmers, emerge from mature colonies, typically in the spring, to mate and establish new colonies. Finding swarmers inside your home or discarded wings near windowsills, doorways, or light fixtures is a strong indication of an active infestation within or very near your property.

    • Swarmers are often mistaken for flying ants, but there are key differences. Termite swarmers have straight antennae, a thick waist, and equally sized wings, while flying ants have elbowed antennae, a pinched waist, and unequal sized wings. Seeing these indicates a well-established colony nearby.

  • TERMITE Frass: This is the waste product of drywood termites. It is small, pellet-like, and often accumulates in small piles near infested wood. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of wood being consumed.

    • Discovering small piles of what looks like sawdust can be a sign of drywood termites working their way through your wooden structures. While less common than subterranean termites in Taylorsville, they can still cause significant localized damage.

  • Clicking Sounds within Walls: Although rare, some people report hearing faint clicking sounds coming from within their walls, which is believed to be the sound of termites chewing through the wood.

    • While not a definitive sign, any unusual sounds within your walls should be investigated, and a professional inspection is highly recommended.

Even if you only suspect you have termites or notice just one of these signs, it is crucial to contact a professional for a thorough inspection. Early detection and intervention are key to minimizing damage and the cost of repairs.

Conditions That Attract Termites

Understanding what attracts termites can help you make your property less appealing to them. Termites are drawn to moisture and wood. Common attractants include:

  • Excess Moisture: Leaky pipes, poor drainage, or standing water near the foundation provide the moisture subterranean termites need to survive. Ensure your gutters are clean and directing water away from your home.

    • Addressing any moisture problems is a critical preventative measure. Repairing leaks promptly and ensuring proper drainage around your home can make a big difference.

  • Wood-to-Soil Contact: Any wood that is in direct contact with the soil provides an easy access point for subterranean termites. This includes wood siding, door frames, and even firewood stored directly on the ground near your house.

    • Creating a barrier between soil and wood is essential. Consider using concrete or metal spacers where wood would otherwise touch the ground and store firewood away from your home on an elevated structure.

  • Mulch and Landscaping: Thick layers of mulch directly against the foundation can retain moisture and provide a food source for termites. Keep mulch several inches away from your foundation.

    • While mulch offers aesthetic benefits, it's important to use it judiciously near your home's foundation to avoid creating a welcoming environment for termites.

  • Cracks in the Foundation: Even small cracks in the foundation can serve as entry points for termites seeking access to the wood within your home.

    • Regularly inspect your foundation for cracks and seal them appropriately. This not only helps prevent termites but also other pests and moisture issues.

  • Firewood and Lumber Piles: Storing firewood or lumber piles directly against your house provides both shelter and a readily available food source for termites.

    • Keep firewood and lumber elevated and stored at least 20 feet away from your home to reduce the risk of attracting termites to your property.

By being aware of these warning signs and reducing the conditions that attract termites, you can significantly lower the risk of an infestation on your Taylorsville property. However, if you suspect you have termites, don't delay in contacting Taylorsville Pest Pros for a professional assessment.

Diligent and Meticulous Preventative Measures Against Termites

Once you've dealt with a termite infestation, or if you want to proactively protect your property, implementing preventative measures is crucial. While professional treatment offers the strongest defense, these steps can significantly reduce the risk of a future infestation in your Taylorsville home or business.

  • Reduce Moisture: Repair leaky plumbing, address poor drainage, and ensure rainwater is diverted away from your foundation. Use dehumidifiers in damp basements or crawl spaces. Ensure your gutters and downspouts are clean and functioning correctly, directing water at least several feet away from the house.

    • Eliminating sources of moisture is one of the most effective ways to deter subterranean termites, as they require moisture to survive and thrive. Simple steps like repairing a dripping faucet can make a difference.

  • Minimize Wood-to-Soil Contact: Keep all wooden elements of your home at least 6 inches above the soil. This includes siding, door frames, and deck supports. For structural elements that must be in contact with the ground, use pressure-treated wood, which is more resistant to termites, although not entirely immune.

    • Creating a physical gap between wood and the soil makes it much harder for subterranean termites to access your main structure. Simple landscaping adjustments can help achieve this barrier.

  • Remove Termite Food Sources: Store firewood and lumber away from your house and elevate it. Remove dead trees, stumps, and excess wood debris from your yard. Termites are attracted to any source of cellulose.

    • Keeping your yard free of tempting wood sources reduces the likelihood of termites establishing colonies near your home and then moving in.

  • Regularly Inspect Your Property: Periodically check for signs of termite activity, such as mud tubes, damaged wood, or discarded wings. Pay close attention to basements, crawl spaces, perimeter walls, and wooden structures like decks and fences. Early detection provides the best chance for effective treatment and minimal damage.

    • Being vigilant and conducting your own inspections can help you spot early signs of a problem before it becomes a full-blown infestation, saving you time, money, and stress.

  • Seal Cracks and Openings: Seal any cracks in your foundation, brickwork, or around utility entry points. This helps prevent termites from entering your home through hidden pathways.

    • Closing off potential entry points makes it more difficult for termites to gain access from the outside, adding another layer of protection to your property.

  • Avoid Using Wood-Based Mulch Right Against the Foundation: While mulch is beneficial for landscaping, keep it a few inches away from your foundation to avoid creating a hospitable environment for termites. Consider using alternatives like gravel or pine needles near the house.

    • Selecting your landscaping materials carefully can contribute to preventing termite problems, particularly around the immediate perimeter of your home.

While these preventative measures are helpful, they do not replace the need for professional treatment if you suspect a termite infestation. If you do find signs of termites, resist the urge to disturb them, as this can cause them to relocate and make treatment more difficult.
