Common Indicators of a Squirrel Infestation

Several tell-tale signs can alert you to the presence of squirrels in or around your home. Knowing what to look and listen for can make all the difference in catching an infestation early. Sometimes these signs overlap with other pest issues, so a professional assessment is always recommended. For instance, the sounds of scurrying can sometimes be mistaken for larger pests if you don't know what specifically to listen for.

Noises in Attics or Walls

One of the most common signs of squirrels is the sound of scurrying, scratching, or gnawing coming from your attic or within your walls, particularly during the daytime. Squirrels are most active during the morning and late afternoon. Hearing these noises is a strong indicator that you have unwanted guests. Unlike rodents which might be more active at night, the daytime sounds are often characteristic of squirrel activity. The sounds can vary in intensity depending on the squirrel's activity level and location. Some people describe it as a rhythmic scratching or a rapid moving sound. This auditory evidence is often the first sign people notice, especially in quieter parts of the day.

Gnaw Marks on Wood or Wiring

Squirrels have a constant need to chew to keep their teeth from overgrowing. They will gnaw on various materials, including wood, insulation, and especially electrical wiring. Finding fresh gnaw marks around entry points, on fascia boards, or near vents is a clear sign of squirrel activity. Chewed electrical wires pose a significant fire risk, making this a serious concern. The damage can range from small nicks to extensive destruction of wood trim or beams. Inspecting vulnerable areas periodically can help identify this damage early. This chewing instinct is a biological necessity for them, but it's incredibly destructive to human structures. The type of material they chew can also give clues about how long they've been present. Fresh gnaw marks indicate recent activity.

Visible Squirrels Entering or Exiting Your Home

Perhaps the most obvious sign is directly observing squirrels entering or exiting your home through specific openings. Watch your roofline, soffits,, and attic vents. If you see squirrels repeatedly going in and out of the same spot, it's a clear indication they have established a den inside. Witnessing this behavior confirms their presence and helps pinpoint their access points. Early morning and late afternoon are prime times to observe this activity. Sometimes they carry nesting materials like leaves or twigs, making their intentions clear. Their ability to squeeze into small spaces is often underestimated until you see them doing it. You might see them running up and down trees and then directly to a point on your roofline. This direct route is a major clue.

Droppings and Urine Stains

Squirrel droppings are typically pellet-shaped. Finding these droppings in your attic, walls, or other areas indicates an infestation. Urine stains can also develop, often accompanied by a strong, unpleasant odor. These are biohazards and require professional cleanup. The accumulation of droppings and urine over time creates unsanitary conditions and can even cause structural damage to wood through prolonged exposure. The smell is often described as musky or pungent and can permeate throughout the home. These signs are often found in areas that are not frequently accessed, like the furthest corners of an attic or crawl space. Proper ant control and cockroach control can also contribute to overall home hygiene, but squirrel waste requires specialized attention.

Damaged insulation not only indicates a pest problem but also reduces your home's energy efficiency.
